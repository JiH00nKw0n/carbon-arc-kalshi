# Experiment Log — 2026-06-03 · Company-level pivot (CA card → company revenue & earnings markets)

**Why this pivot.** The macro thesis (CA alt-data → US macro prints, traded on Kalshi) was a
comprehensive, well-powered NULL (see `test_2026-06-02.md`). Team call (Slack): a macro print is
driven by countless factors and CA is a tiny slice → low signal ratio + ~one cycle. **Fix: change Y
from macro to COMPANY level** (keep X = CA alone). Classic, still-valid alt-data use =
**credit-card transactions → company revenue**. Card spend is a LARGE fraction of a consumer
company's revenue (high signal ratio), and companies × quarters = big N. Two parallel tracks
(둘 병행): **A** fundamentals (revenue), **B** Polymarket/Kalshi company markets.

**Data bought ($293.41 of $300 budget; promo balance $10,133).** One CA0056 framework, **35 consumer
tickers** (insight 626 card spend), quarterly, US, 2023–2026 → `outputs/auto/ca0056_card_spend_by_ticker_q_3y.csv`
(950 rows, 35 tickers, Online/Physical). Y actuals: FMP `/stable/income-statement` quarterly revenue,
35 tickers → `outputs/auto/fmp_revenue_by_ticker.csv` (698 rows). Scripts: `s_p_revenue_nowcast.py`
(Track A), Track B pilot inline → `outputs/auto/trackB_beat_pilot.csv`.
Method: align CA(calendar-q) ↔ revenue(fiscal-q) by nearest date ≤50d; YoY (÷4q); company-clustered
bootstrap + shuffle-company surrogate.

## Track A — Fundamentals: does CA card spend measure company revenue?

### H1 — CA card-spend LEVEL tracks revenue level (within company). ✅ STRONG
- Per-company median r = **+0.80**; 94% of companies r>0; pooled (company-clustered) r=**+0.93** (p<0.001).
- ⇒ The card panel genuinely captures a company's sales level. (Contrast: at macro level even this failed.)

### H2 — CA YoY growth tracks revenue YoY growth (the useful nowcast). ✅ SUPPORTED
- Per-company median r = **+0.46**; 89% positive; pooled r=**+0.50**, p_boot<0.001, **p_surrogate=0.000**
  (survives shuffle-company null). Effect is real, not a pooling artifact.

### H3 — Link is stronger for high-card-share sectors. ✅ (mechanism-consistent)
- travel/gig **+0.89** · apparel/specialty **+0.73** · restaurant **+0.61** · big-box **+0.54** ·
  ecom/digital **+0.46** · **discount +0.09** (DG/DLTR/KR — cash/SNAP/membership heavy → card misses).
- Per-company extremes fit the mechanism: ROST +0.96, MCD +0.93, CMG +0.90 (high card share) vs
  COST −0.67, AAPL −0.55 (membership / big-ticket non-card / services → card unrepresentative).

### H4 — Does CA LEAD, or just co-move? ⚠️ nowcast yes, "lead" no
- contemp CA_yoy(Q) vs rev_yoy(Q) r=+0.50 ≈ lagged CA_yoy(Q−1) r=+0.49. No resolvable *lead*.
- BUT CA for quarter Q is available ~weeks BEFORE that quarter's earnings print → contemporaneous-quarter
  CA is already a usable **nowcast** (you know CA before the revenue is reported).

**Track A verdict: the mechanism is REAL** — CA card spend measures consumer-company revenue (level r≈0.8,
growth r≈0.5, surrogate-clean), strongly for high-card-share sectors. This is the first genuinely
positive, well-powered result in the whole project (macro was null on even this).

## Track B — Company prediction markets (Polymarket)

### H_B0 — Do tradeable company markets exist for our tickers? ✅ YES (rich)
- Polymarket has **"Will [company] beat quarterly earnings?"** markets for **31 of our 35 tickers**
  (MCD/NKE/SBUX/LULU/URBN/WMT/…), multiple quarters each; resolved via UMA with clean outcomes. Also
  stock-price & KPI markets (AAPL close, Tesla deliveries, Amazon capex). Read access is keyless.

### H_B1 — Does CA card growth predict the earnings BEAT (vs consensus)? ❌ NULL
- 81 resolved beat-markets × CA card YoY: corr(CA_yoy, beat) = **−0.15** (wrong sign); beat rate
  CA_yoy>0: 0.66 vs CA_yoy<0: 0.70; **surrogate p=0.16**. No predictive signal.
- **Interpretation — the key distinction (recurring theme):** CA *measures* revenue (Track A ✓), but a
  "beat" is the residual of actual vs **analyst consensus**, and consensus already prices the demand
  trend CA carries (analysts use card data too). **Measuring the quantity ≠ having edge over the market
  that prices it.** (Also: "beat" is usually EPS — margins/buybacks — doubly removed from card-revenue.)

## EDGE tests (the rigorous follow-up) — DECISIVE NULL

After H1/H2 (measurement) and H_B1 (crude beat proxy), we ran the *meaningful* edge tests: does CA carry
info **not already in price/consensus**, observable before the print? Signal `CA_surprise(Q)` = CA card
YoY for the reported quarter, residualized vs the company's own trailing-4q CA-growth (self-contained,
known at quarter-end before earnings). 240 earnings events, 35 tickers, 2024-10…2026-05.
(`s_q_edge_fetch.py` + `s_q_edge_tests.py`, `docs/analysis_company_edge.md`.)

| Hypothesis | Test | Result | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **H1 (money)** | CA_surprise → earnings-day mkt-adj return | r=−0.08, p_surr=0.28; hit-rate 55.8%; L/S earnings-day PnL t=−0.27 | ❌ null |
| **H2 (analysts)** | CA_surprise → EPS surprise (actual−est) | r=+0.02, p_boot=0.49, p_surr=0.74 | ❌ null |
| **H3 (rigorous, the "causality" gap)** | OOS Diebold-Mariano: rev_yoy forecast AR(1) vs +CA | RMSE 0.075→**0.088** (worse), DM=−0.88, p=0.38 | ❌ null (CA adds nothing OOS) |
| **H_REV (clean info test)** | CA_surprise → REVENUE surprise (actual − point-in-time consensus, FMP revenueEstimated) | r=−0.07, p_boot=0.25, **p_surr=0.11**; raw ca_yoy r=+0.08 | ❌ null |
| **H4 (where)** | H1/H2/H_REV by card-share tercile & coverage | no tier signal (high-cardshare H_REV r=+0.01); lone flickers wrong-signed = noise | ❌ null |

> **H_REV note (the last gap, now closed):** point-in-time *revenue* consensus came from FMP
> `/stable/earnings` (`revenueEstimated` vs `revenueActual`), not FactSet — cleaner than the EPS proxy.
> CA still does not predict the revenue surprise. **Extra finding (sanity):** revenue_surprise →
> earnings-day return is itself weak (r=−0.06) for these consumer names — EPS/margins/guidance drive the
> reaction, not the revenue beat. So even a perfect revenue-surprise predictor wouldn't cleanly trade.
> (Caveat: rev-surprise sd≈1.07 is noisy — some `revenueEstimated` scale issues — but the high-card-share
> tier, which should be cleanest, is flat r=+0.01, so the null holds.)

**Why these and not Granger/CCF:** CA card spend and revenue *co-measure* the same consumer spending —
"who leads whom" is the wrong frame (category error). The correct rigorous test is **OOS incremental
prediction (Diebold-Mariano) vs a no-CA baseline + market/consensus-relative** — H3 + H1/H2. All null.

**EDGE verdict: CA has NO tradeable edge at the company level.** It *measures* revenue (Track A,
near-tautological since card spend ⊂ revenue) but contains **no information beyond what price and
analyst consensus already embed** — it does not predict earnings-day returns, EPS surprises, or improve
OOS revenue forecasts. The market already prices the card signal (consistent with card data being a
widely-used, ~decade-old alt-data source). This is the same lesson as macro, now confirmed at company
level with the rigorous tests: **measuring ≠ beating the market.**

## ⭐ CORRECTION (FactSet point-in-time consensus) — the info layer is NOT null

Earlier I concluded "CA carries no info beyond consensus" from H_REV using **FMP** `revenueEstimated` —
but that estimate is noisy (surprise sd≈1.07; scale issues), which drowned any signal. Redone with
**FactSet FE_BASIC_CONH_QF point-in-time SALES consensus** (surprise sd=0.030, clean) and the methodology
fixes the user insisted on — **X = ca_yoy (CLEAN, no consensus subtraction → no shared-term artifact),
consensus measured as of CA-availability (t_ca = fiscal-q-end+7d)** — the result changes
(`s_t_revsurprise_factset.py`, n=313):

| test | r | p_surr | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| ca_yoy → revenue surprise (vs t_ca consensus) | **+0.192** | **0.008** | ✅ real, modest |
| ca_yoy → revenue surprise (vs pre-print consensus) | +0.152 | 0.029 | ✅ (early≈print) |
| (ca_yoy − consensus) → surprise [artifact check] | +0.05 | — | artifact gone with clean data |

**So CA DOES carry modest revenue information beyond analyst consensus** (r≈0.19 = ~3.6% of surprise
variance; surrogate-clean; sign correct). This **overturns my earlier null** — it was an FMP-data-quality
artifact, surfaced only because the user pushed for clean point-in-time consensus + artifact-free X.

**BUT it still does not make money** (the edge/return layer stays null):
- revenue surprise → earnings-day return: r=+0.07 (weak) — for these consumer names the stock reacts to
  **EPS / margins / guidance**, not the revenue beat.
- (ca − consensus) → earnings-day return: r=+0.04, p_surr=0.52 → null.

**Net (corrected):** CA has a *genuine but small* revenue-nowcast edge over analyst consensus — it is
NOT merely a measurement fully embedded in consensus. However that revenue edge does **not** translate
into tradeable equity earnings-day returns, because revenue surprise barely drives the stock (EPS/
guidance dominate). Tradeability would need a revenue-specific instrument (e.g. a revenue-beat market)
or a multi-factor model — not the equity earnings-day move.

## Caveats
- Window 2023–2026 (~13 q/ticker; no COVID). Beat markets are recent → only ~3 q/ticker, n=81 (modest power; p=0.16 can't strongly reject either way, but no positive sign).
- CA(calendar-q)↔revenue(fiscal-q) nearest-date alignment is approximate for offset-fiscal names (WMT/COST/NKE); YoY mitigates.
- "Beat" = EPS beat (not pure revenue) for most Polymarket markets → noisy proxy for revenue surprise.
- Card panel unrepresentative for membership (COST), services/big-ticket (AAPL), cash/SNAP (discount).

## ⭐ FOLLOW-UP (2026-06-04) — "매출 말고 EPS는 못 맞추나?" → NO (clean, decisive)

Ran the EPS analogue of the revenue test with **identical rigor** (`scripts/auto/s_u_epssurprise_factset.py`):
same clean X (`ca_yoy`), same 35 tickers, **FactSet point-in-time EPS consensus** (`FE_BASIC_CONH_QF`,
`FE_ITEM='EPS'`), CONS_EARLY ≈ as-of fiscal-quarter-end, CONS_PRINT ≈ last pre-report. EPS-specific
handling: denominator `|consensus|`, drop `|EPS|<0.05`, surprise winsorized at 1/99%. n=304 events.

| target (same X = ca_yoy clean) | r | p_boot | p_surr | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **revenue** surprise_early (point-in-time) | **+0.19** | — | **0.008** | ✅ real, modest |
| **EPS** surprise_early (point-in-time) | **+0.065** | 0.302 | **0.235** | ❌ null |
| EPS surprise_print (point-in-time) | +0.057 | 0.418 | 0.391 | ❌ null |
| (ca − cons) → earnings-day return | −0.048 | 0.371 | 0.439 | ❌ null |

Sanity checks confirm the test has power and isn't broken:
- EPS surprise → earnings-day return **r=+0.146** (the market *does* react to EPS surprises — unlike
  revenue surprises at r=0.07; this is exactly why EPS is what matters for the stock).
- EPS beat-rate 74% (consensus runs conservative; consistent with reality, so the consensus is real).

**Why CA predicts revenue (r=0.19) but NOT EPS (r=0.065), in one line:** CA card spend is a **top-line /
demand proxy**. EPS = revenue − COGS − opex − interest − tax ± one-offs ± buybacks. Everything CA cannot
see (margins, cost control, share count, tax, write-offs) sits between revenue and EPS, and *that* is
where the EPS surprise is actually generated. So the modest revenue edge gets washed out by the time it
reaches the bottom line.

**The bitter irony for tradeability:** the line CA *can* nowcast (revenue) barely moves the stock
(r=0.07); the line that *does* move the stock (EPS, r=0.15) is the one CA *cannot* nowcast (r=0.065).
The signal and the payout don't overlap. This is the cleanest single-sentence statement of why
company-level CA has no standalone earnings-day equity edge.

Polymarket "Will X beat quarterly earnings?" resolves YES iff **non-GAAP EPS > consensus**. So the clean,

superseded the first-pass −0.15. Same root cause as the EPS-surprise null: CA can't see the margin/cost
line that decides an EPS beat.

### Polymarket + Kalshi reconnaissance (live markets)
- **Polymarket**: the per-company EPS-beat format is brand-new & thin — only **CPB** in the searchable
  cache ($25 vol), not in our 35. Also exist: "[co] Up/Down After Earnings" (= a market form of H1, but
  on tech names, not ours) and a few revenue-side metric markets (**DAL PRASM, DAL load factor, Ulta comp
  sales**) — but 1 quarter each, future-dated → **no historical panel to test**. The thin price (≤$25) is
  not an efficient benchmark anyway, so a market-PRICE edge test is not feasible.
- **Kalshi**: **NO company quarterly earnings/revenue-beat markets exist.** Company category = AGI,
  M&A/acquisitions, layoffs, IPO underwriters, US-gov stakes, AI-model rankings, streaming charts. Closest
  are "Will the SEC drop quarterly reporting?" (regulatory) and macro GDP. → no analog to run.
- **Net**: the only liquid, historical "beat" signal resolves on **EPS**, which s_u + this H_B1 already
  cover far more rigorously than any thin market could. Nothing tradeable for CA's one real edge (revenue),
  because no liquid **revenue-beat** market exists.

| X transform | r | n | p_boot | p_surr |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## ⭐ EPS DECOMPOSITION + AUGMENTATION (2026-06-04) — `s_aa`, `s_ab`

**Structural build-up (`s_aa_eps_decomp.py`):** EPS ≈ (revenue − cost)/shares, so test the chain.
- corr(rev_surprise, eps_surprise)=+0.231 but regression R²=**0.053** → only **~5% of the EPS surprise is
  revenue-driven; ~95% is margin/cost/other.**
- ca_yoy→rev_surprise=+0.234 (real) ; cost_yoy→margin_resid=+0.019 (p_surr=0.77, **null**) ; ca_yoy→eps_surprise
  =+0.065 ≈ 0.234×0.231 (the direct null IS the product of the two weak links).
- Multivariate `eps_surprise ~ ca_yoy+cost_yoy`: R²=0.006. Ceiling (perfect revenue info): R²=0.052.
- ⇒ even assembled structurally, CA can't reach EPS: revenue link too weak to amplify, margin term invisible to CA.

**Augment CA with free pre-earnings data (`s_ab_augmented_eps.py`):** added ANALYST ESTIMATE DYNAMICS from
FactSet (revision momentum, breadth = up−down analysts, dispersion) — all point-in-time pre-print. n=310.

| signal (Y=EPS surprise) | r | p_surr |
|---|---|---|
| ca_yoy (CA card) | +0.066 | 0.228 (null) |
| eps_rev (revision momentum, 90d) | +0.158 | 0.024 ✅ |
| **eps_breadth (analysts up−down)** | **+0.149** | **0.005 ✅ (cleanest)** |
| eps_disp (dispersion) | +0.137 | 0.031 ✅ |

Nested R²: card-only **0.004** → estimate-dynamics-only **0.052** → card+dynamics **0.054**. CA's increment
beyond estimate dynamics: r=−0.032, p_surr=0.544 → **CA adds nothing.**

**Conclusions:**
1. Free **analyst estimate dynamics DO see the margin/EPS part CA can't** (R² 0.004→0.052, surrogate-clean) —
   the right answer to "what augments CA for EPS." Best single = **revision breadth**.
2. But **CA does not augment them** — CA is redundant once you have estimate dynamics (incremental p_surr=0.54).
   For EPS, CA is the weak link, not the missing piece.
3. Even the best free signals explain only **~5% of EPS-surprise variance** → ~95% is genuinely unpredictable
   pre-print. And estimate dynamics are PUBLIC (revision-drift/under-reaction anomaly) → small known edge, not CA edge.
4. Net: CA's unique value remains the semi-independent **revenue** read (r≈0.19). For **EPS**, public analyst
   dynamics dominate and CA contributes nothing incremental.

## ⭐ DELIVERABLE (2026-06-04) — multifactor REVENUE-surprise nowcast (`s_ac_revenue_nowcast.py`)

Built the thing CA is actually good for. Target = revenue surprise (actual − point-in-time consensus)/cons.
