4. LLM кЈЉк±∞ (л∞ЬмЈМ)

card

metric: Consumer card spend is most powerful when the reported metric is also a dollar measure of consumer transactions. It is strongest for revenue growth levels, still useful for revenue or GMV surprises where consensus has not fully incorporated actual quarter spending, and weaker for normalized operating metrics that require denominators the signal does not observe, such as same-store definitions, square footage, or hotel rooms occupied.
company: I rank the signal highest where consumer credit/debit card dollars are the economic transaction that becomes reported quarterly revenue: specialty retail, grocery, restaurants, and consumer leisure with little lag. I downgrade companies where card spend is a gross-volume proxy rather than revenue, where revenue is royalty/fee/take-rate based, where bookings are recognized later, where B2B/wholesale/franchise/international channels are large, or where payments occur through app stores, subscriptions, ACH, insurance, cash, or corporate accounts. The key distinction is whether the card data measures the same dollar that analysts are forecasting as revenue, not merely whether consumers interact with the brand.

foot

metric: Mobile geolocation foot traffic is a physical-visit volume signal. It is strongest for operating metrics whose numerator is in-store activity and weakest when the target is consensus-adjusted, value-based, online, or dependent on ticket size and mix. Therefore it should best predict actual same-store sales and revenue growth, retain some but weaker power for early surprises and sales-density surprises, and have little incremental power for print-time revenue surprise, hotel occupancy surprise, or GMV.
company: Mobile geolocation foot traffic is most predictive when reported revenue is mainly physical-location transaction volume with relatively stable ticket and limited non-store channels. It is strongest for company-operated restaurants and store-dependent off-price/value retailers. It becomes only moderate when ticket size, promotions, e-commerce, franchising, fuel/pharmacy, global exposure, or analyst-visible public demand data dilute the incremental information. It is weakest where visits do not map cleanly to reported revenue, such as pharmacies, auto parts commercial delivery, home-improvement project tickets, casinos, and refining.

click

metric: Clickstream is most powerful when the measured website visit is economically close to the revenue-generating action. Therefore it should work best for online revenue growth and marketplace GMV, somewhat less well for revenue surprises after consensus is considered, and only weakly for offline operating metrics. Metrics that are store-density or store-comps surprises require the web signal to proxy physical behavior and also beat consensus, making them much less predictable.
company: This ranking prioritizes businesses where monthly web visitors are closest to the economic transaction that creates revenue: online retail, e-commerce marketplaces, OTAs, and ticketing marketplaces rank highest because a visit often represents purchase intent and can convert within the quarter.
